Introduction

Cancer biotechnology is a specialized branch of biotechnology that focuses on understanding, diagnosing, and treating cancer using advanced biological techniques. Cancer occurs due to uncontrolled cell division caused by genetic mutations. Biotechnology helps in studying cancer at the molecular level and developing targeted therapies.

Types of Cancer

  • Carcinoma: Cancer originating from epithelial cells.
  • Sarcoma: Cancer of connective tissues like bone and muscle.
  • Leukemia: Cancer of blood-forming tissues.
  • Lymphoma: Cancer of the lymphatic system.
  • Myeloma: Cancer affecting plasma cells.

Role of Cell Culture in Cancer Research

Cell culture is an essential tool in cancer biotechnology. Cancer cells are grown in controlled laboratory conditions to study their growth, behavior, and response to drugs.

  • Understanding cancer cell division
  • Drug screening and toxicity testing
  • Studying gene expression in cancer cells
  • Developing personalized cancer treatments

Biotechnological Techniques Used

  • Recombinant DNA Technology: Production of therapeutic proteins
  • Monoclonal Antibody Technology: Targeted cancer therapy
  • CRISPR-Cas9: Gene editing for cancer research
  • Bioinformatics: Cancer genome analysis
  • Flow Cytometry: Analysis of cancer cell populations

Biotechnology-Based Cancer Therapies

  • Targeted drug therapy
  • Immunotherapy
  • Gene therapy
  • Cancer vaccines
  • Personalized medicine

Future Scope of Cancer Biotechnology

The future of cancer biotechnology is promising with advancements in gene editing, artificial intelligence, and personalized medicine. These innovations aim to provide more effective, less toxic, and patient-specific cancer treatments.
